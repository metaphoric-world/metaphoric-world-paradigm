
### Appendix 2 - Time and Causality across species

This appendix provides a comparative overview of how different organisms perceive time and causal structure.  
It connects the Plateau‑model of world‑formation (Chapter 18) with the claims made in Chapter 19 about semantic time, temporal distortion, and the emergence of causal reasoning.

The goal is not to offer an exhaustive zoological catalogue, but to show how empirical findings align with the topological structure of the Metaphoric World.

I - Time perception across evolutionary plateaus

*Plateau 0 - Chemical Systems*

No time.  

Chemical systems respond to gradients but do not accumulate drift or generate expectations.  
There is no subjective duration, no memory, no anticipation.  
Only reaction rates exist.

This corresponds to the absence of semantic time in Chapter 19.

*Plateau 1 - Proto‑Worlds (Plants, Algae, Single Cells)*

Minimal temporal structure. 

Plants and unicellular organisms show:
- circadian rhythms  
- entrainment to light cycles  
- slow adaptive drift  

But they do not represent duration or sequence.  
There is no subjective time, only periodic modulation.

This matches the claim that semantic time requires recursive structuring of distinctions.

*Plateau 2 - Meta‑Patterns (Insects)*

Proto‑temporal cognition. 

Insects exhibit surprisingly rich temporal abilities:
- interval timing (seconds to minutes)  
- circadian and ultradian rhythms  
- time‑dependent navigation (e.g., bee dance)  
- learned expectations about delay and reward  

However, insects show no evidence of:
- subjective time dilation  
- affective modulation of duration  
- narrative or episodic time  
- temporal fragmentation  

Their time is structured, but not experienced.

This aligns with Chapter 19:  
semantic time (stretching, compression, folding) does not appear until Plateau 3.

*Plateau 3 - Intentionality (Mammals, Birds, Octopuses)*

Full semantic time without symbolic recursion. 

Here we see the first clear evidence of subjective temporal modulation:
- Time dilation under fear  
  (rodents, dogs, elephants, primates)  
- Time compression under high engagement  
  (predatory birds, dolphins, felines)  
- Flow‑like states  
  (play behavior in mammals and corvids)  
- Trauma‑induced temporal fragmentation  
  (elephants, dogs, primates)  
- Episodic‑like memory  
  (scrub jays, rats, octopuses)  
- Future planning  
  (corvids, apes, cephalopods)

These findings map directly onto the claims in Chapter 19:
- boredom stretches time  
- flow collapses time  
- trauma fragments time  
- memory folds time  
- meaning condenses time  

Plateau 3 organisms have semantic time, but not symbolic time.

*Plateau 4 - Symbolic Worlds (Humans)*

Narrative time, historical time, biographical time.
  
Humans add:
- symbolic recursion  
- cultural time  
- calendrical time  
- counterfactual time  
- metaphysical time  
- narrative coherence  

This is the full form of semantic time described in Chapter 19.

*Plateau (?) - Machine Systems (LLMs)*

No time.  

LLMs have:
- no affective gradients  
- no subjective duration  
- no continuity of perspective  
- no temporal self‑anchoring  

They operate in context windows, not in time.

This is consistent with Chapter 19’s claim that semantic time requires affective coupling and recursive world‑formation.

II - Causality across species

*Plateau 0–1: No Causality*

Chemical and proto‑world systems show:
- no causal inference  
- no counterfactual reasoning  
- no expectation models  

Only direct coupling to gradients.

*Plateau 2: Sensorimotor Causality (Insects)*

Insects exhibit:
- learned contingencies (“if I do X, Y happens”)  
- simple tool use in some species (ants)  
- predictive navigation (bees)  

But they do not show:
- abstract causal reasoning  
- physical intuition  
- social causality  
- intentional causality  

Their causality is procedural, not conceptual.

*Plateau 3: Physical and Social Causality (Mammals, Birds, Octopuses)*

These animals show:
- intuitive physics (gravity, solidity, support)  
- tool use and tool innovation  
- causal inference from observation  
- social causality (dominance, intention, deception)  
- counterfactual reasoning in limited form  

This matches the emergence of semantic time:  
causality becomes a coherence pattern, not a chain.

*Plateau 4: Symbolic Causality (Humans)*

Humans add:
- narrative causality  
- metaphysical causality  
- legal causality  
- scientific causality  
- counterfactual depth  

This is the full symbolic elaboration of the coherence gradients described in Chapter 19.

IV - Integration with Chapter 19

The empirical record supports the central claims of Chapter 19:

1. Semantic time is not universal.  
It emerges only when a system can recursively structure distinctions and modulate them affectively.

2. Time distortion is not uniquely human.  
Plateau‑3 animals show clear evidence of temporal stretching, compression, and fragmentation.

3. Causality is not fundamental.  
It appears gradually as a coherence pattern, not as a metaphysical chain.

4. Symbolic time and symbolic causality are uniquely human.  
They arise only at Plateau 4, where language enables recursive projection.

5. The field model predicts these transitions.  
Each plateau corresponds to a deeper stabilization of actualization patterns in the semantic‑topological field.
