
### Closing words

I wonder what belongs here, what may help the Metaphoric World come to live, or what might even damage what i tried to build before.

What comes to mind is the cultural heritage this work builds on. I don't mean German history specifically here, more my "personal board of inspiration". Some have been named in the text, like Bertrand Russell, Luc Ciompi, Kurt Gödel, Pieter Bruegel the Elder or Thomas Pynchon - all male.

Several women thinkers - Ada Lovelace, Emmy Noether, Sophie Germain, Hildegard von Bingen, Emily Dickinson, Annette Messager, and others - anticipated aspects of the field‑architecture in poetic, mathematical, or artistic form.
Their work resonates with the Metaphoric World,
even though historical constraints prevented them
from entering the formal domains in which this architecture is developed. 
It's overdue that they (slowly, alas) enter the focus of discourse. Women thinkers were under represented in my personal past too, and there was little impulse to actively seek them out. This is an explanation, not an excuse.
What really matters to me now is my wish that the Metaphoric World can help build a non-exclusive future.

On the use of AI and the open structure of this book

This book rests entirely on my own theoretical reflections, models, and structural analyses - as far as any human can claim such a thing. Without the people before and beside me, none of this would exist. (In this sense, this does equally include people like Bertrand Russell, the mother of our son, and the unknown bus driver on my way to an important appointment in the past.)
As I wrote in the preface: I did not invent this structure - I merely uncovered it.

At several points LLM tools were used to check considerations, or to simply expand them into readable text. To move beyond the first version -basically a text container with conceptual sketches- required a lot of structural reorganisation, deepening and expansion.
My stance on this has (hopefully) been made clear in the corresponding chapter - not a biased blindness to current problems, but an openness to the technology itself and its (real) possibilities.

This also means there was no time to turn the text into a piece of literature. 
(Since i'm able to do this as well in principle, future refinements might fullfill such needs. Stay attuned.)

However, everything has been examined for coherence and integrated into the broader argument; illustrative details do not carry or determine the theoretical framework. 
I'm aware that this still might sound heretical to people socialized in the paradigm of linear logic - but the Metaphoric World doesn't reside in an ontology where Popper's falsification is applicable.

The Metaphoric World is not a closed system. 
Its central property are the motifs of metaphor as resonant transmission, of structural compatibility and of ambiguity as a resource. In this sense, errors are constructive. They do not weaken the structure; they open new questions, point toward what has not yet been fully seen, and invite further refinement.
The first version wasn't even planned, and certainly not built from scrap. It was closer to the famous Heureka! (minus the genius) - structures of thought aggregated over decades finally started to make sense as a whole. So it may or may not surprise that only four months have passed since the first release.

This book is therefore not an endpoint but a beginning. Every reader is invited to think along, to extend, to question, to adjust, to deepen.
The Metaphoric World lives through connection - and connection emerges only when many voices enter into resonance.
